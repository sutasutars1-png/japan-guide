Kanazawa

Kyoto's beauty without Kyoto's crowds

Best time to visit: Apr–May, Nov; snowy Jan gardens

Spared by war, Kanazawa preserves samurai lanes, geisha districts and one of Japan's three great gardens — Kenroku-en — with a fraction of Kyoto's visitor numbers. The Higashi Chaya teahouse district glows gold at dusk (fittingly: the city produces 99% of Japan's gold leaf, which locals put on everything from crafts to ice cream).

Omicho Market serves some of the country's best seafood bowls thanks to the cold Sea of Japan, and the 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art gives the old town a modern pulse. The Hokuriku Shinkansen makes it an easy 2.5-hour hop from Tokyo.

Through the seasons

Kenroku-en's plum and cherry seasons run February–April, and the garden opens free with evening illuminations during peak sakura week.

Green season: moss and maples shade the samurai district, and the Hyakumangoku Festival (early June) parades lords and lion dances through town.

Snow-hanging yukitsuri ropes go up in Kenroku-en from November — a photogenic craft in itself — while crab season opens November 6 with fanfare.

The definitive Kanazawa: snow on yukitsuri pines, steaming kaisendon at Omicho Market, and kanburi (winter yellowtail) and snow crab at their absolute peak.

Don't miss

  • Kenroku-en, one of Japan's three great gardens
  • Higashi Chaya geisha district at dusk
  • Seafood bowls at Omicho Market
  • Gold-leaf everything, including ice cream
  • 21st Century Museum's 'Swimming Pool'

Getting there

From Tokyo: Hokuriku Shinkansen, ~2h30m. From Kyoto/Osaka: Thunderbird + Shinkansen via Tsuruga, ~2h.
