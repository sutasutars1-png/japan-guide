Hiroshima & Miyajima

A moving peace memorial and a floating torii gate

Best time to visit: Mar–May, Oct–Nov

Hiroshima pairs two of Japan's most powerful experiences in a single trip. The Peace Memorial Park and Museum tell the story of August 6, 1945 with restraint and humanity; nearly every visitor calls it the most affecting museum they have ever entered. The city around it is lively and forward-looking, famous for okonomiyaki layered with noodles.

A 40-minute tram-and-ferry ride away, Miyajima island's Itsukushima Shrine appears to float on the sea at high tide, its great vermilion torii one of Japan's defining images. Stay for sunset after the day-trippers leave.

Through the seasons

Peace Memorial Park's riverbanks bloom with cherry trees, and Miyajima's blossoms frame the floating torii — a gentle, moving season to visit.

August 6 memorial ceremonies and evening lantern floating on the rivers are profound; Miyajima's Kangensai boat festival brings court music to the bay.

Momijidani ('Maple Valley') on Miyajima earns its name in mid-late November; maple-leaf momiji manju cakes taste best fresh off the griddle.

Oyster season peaks — Miyajima's grilled-oyster stalls and Hiroshima's kaki-nabe hot pots are the draw; sights are blissfully uncrowded.

In pictures

Don't miss

  • Peace Memorial Park, Museum and the A-Bomb Dome
  • Itsukushima Shrine's floating torii at high tide
  • Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki at Okonomimura
  • Mt. Misen ropeway and wild deer on Miyajima
  • Oyster shacks along Miyajima's shopping street

Getting there

From Osaka: Sanyo Shinkansen, ~80–90 min. Miyajima: tram/JR to Miyajimaguchi + 10-min ferry.
