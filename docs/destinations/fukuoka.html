福Kyushu

Fukuoka

Yatai street stalls, tonkotsu ramen and easygoing Kyushu charm

Best time to visit: Mar–May, Oct–Nov

Fukuoka is the closest major Japanese city to Korea and Taiwan, and it shows in its open, unhurried character. The city's signature is the yatai: around a hundred open-air food stalls that appear at dusk along the Nakasu and Tenjin riversides, serving tonkotsu ramen, yakitori and cold beer shoulder-to-shoulder with locals.

By day, browse Kushida Shrine and Canal City, or take easy side trips to the torii-lined shrine of Dazaifu, the hells of Beppu, or Kumamoto Castle. Fukuoka's airport is famously 11 minutes by subway from downtown — no city in Japan is easier to arrive in.

Through the seasons

Maizuru Park's castle-ruin sakura peak late March with night illuminations, and Dazaifu's 6,000 plum trees bloom even earlier (February).

Hakata Gion Yamakasa (July 1–15) climaxes with one-ton floats raced through dawn streets — Fukuoka's soul on full display; yatai stalls stay lively all night.

Perfect yatai weather returns; November's grand sumo tournament comes to town, and Dazaifu's ginkgo and maples color the shrine approach.

Motsunabe and mizutaki hot pot season — Fukuoka's soul food shines. Mild winters make it Japan's easiest big city for December–February travel.

In pictures

Don't miss

  • Yatai stall-hopping along the Nakasu river
  • Original tonkotsu ramen (Ichiran & Ippudo were born here)
  • Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine day trip
  • Ohori Park and Fukuoka Castle ruins
  • Mentaiko (spicy cod roe) in every form

Getting there

Fukuoka Airport → Hakata Station: subway, 11 min. From Osaka: Sanyo Shinkansen, ~2h30m. Ferries connect to Busan, Korea.
