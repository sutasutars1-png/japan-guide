Sapporo & Hokkaido

Powder snow, miso ramen and wide-open wilderness

Best time to visit: Dec–Feb (snow), Jun–Aug (lavender, cool summers)

Hokkaido is Japan at a different scale: dairy farms, volcanic lakes, and ski resorts like Niseko whose 'Japow' powder snow draws skiers from around the world. Sapporo, the island's easygoing capital, is the gateway — famous for miso ramen, soup curry, the February Snow Festival's giant ice sculptures, and beer that carries the city's name.

In summer the island flips: lavender fields in Furano, flower carpets in Biei, and temperatures a merciful 10°C cooler than Tokyo. Fresh uni, crab and scallops are reason enough for the flight north.

Through the seasons

Sakura arrives late (early May) — Hokkaido's bloom coincides with Golden Week — and Shibazakura pink moss carpets Higashimokoto from mid-May.

The escape season: 25°C days, Furano lavender peaking mid-July, Biei's patchwork hills, and beer gardens filling Odori Park all August.

Japan's earliest koyo — Daisetsuzan peaks turn crimson mid-September; the Autumn Fest (Sept) turns Odori Park into a giant Hokkaido food fair.

The main event: Sapporo Snow Festival's giant sculptures (early Feb), Otaru's candlelit Snow Light Path, and Niseko powder that skiers cross oceans for.

In pictures

Don't miss

  • Sapporo Snow Festival (early February)
  • Miso ramen in Susukino's Ramen Alley
  • Skiing Niseko's world-famous powder
  • Otaru canal town day trip
  • Furano lavender & Biei hills in summer

Getting there

Fly Tokyo→New Chitose, ~95 min, then rapid train to Sapporo, 40 min. Winter travelers: book flights and Niseko lodging early.
