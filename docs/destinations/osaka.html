阪Kansai

Osaka

Japan's kitchen — loud, friendly and outrageously delicious

Best time to visit: Mar–May, Sep–Nov

Osakans have a word — kuidaore — that means 'to eat yourself into ruin', and the city takes it seriously. Dotonbori's neon riverfront is the postcard, but the eating is best in Tenma's alley izakaya, Kuromon Market, and the takoyaki stands of Shinsekai. People here talk to strangers, haggle, and laugh loudly; after reserved Tokyo, Osaka feels like a warm bath.

It is also the most convenient base in Kansai: Kyoto, Nara and Kobe are each under 45 minutes away, and Universal Studios Japan (with Super Nintendo World) anchors the bay area.

Through the seasons

Osaka Castle Park's 3,000 cherry trees and the Kema Sakuranomiya riverside are prime hanami; the Mint Bureau opens its rare late-blooming avenue in mid-April.

Tenjin Matsuri (July 24–25), one of Japan's three great festivals, ends with a river procession and fireworks; beat the heat in USJ's night sessions.

Comfortable eating weather — Minoo Park's waterfall maples are a 30-minute train ride, with fried maple-leaf tempura as the local snack.

Kushikatsu and kitsune udon weather. The Festival of the Lights turns Midosuji Boulevard into a 4-km illumination, and crowds at USJ hit their yearly low.

Don't miss

  • Dotonbori neon river walk and the Glico sign
  • Kuromon Ichiba Market street food
  • Osaka Castle and its park
  • Universal Studios Japan & Super Nintendo World
  • Retro Shinsekai district and Tsutenkaku Tower

Getting there

From Kansai Airport: Nankai Rapi:t or JR, ~40–50 min. From Tokyo: Shinkansen to Shin-Osaka, ~2h30m.
