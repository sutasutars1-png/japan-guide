Hakone & Mt. Fuji

Hot springs, ropeways and the perfect Fuji view

Best time to visit: Oct–Feb for clearest Fuji views

Hakone is Tokyo's classic overnight escape: a volcanic caldera of hot-spring ryokan, forest shrines and open-air art, 90 minutes from Shinjuku. The looping course — mountain railway, cable car, ropeway over the steaming Owakudani valley, then a 'pirate ship' across Lake Ashi — is a full day of fun and, on clear days, delivers postcard views of Mt. Fuji.

Stay one night in a ryokan with an onsen bath and kaiseki dinner; it is many travelers' single favorite night in Japan. For closer Fuji views, the Kawaguchiko lake area and the Chureito Pagoda are a separate, equally rewarding trip.

Through the seasons

Late-blooming mountain sakura frame Fuji into early May; the Fuji Shibazakura Festival carpets the lakes area pink from mid-April.

Official Mt. Fuji climbing season (July–early Sept) and hydrangeas along the Hakone Tozan Railway; expect clouds hiding Fuji by midday.

Silver pampas grass turns the Sengokuhara hills to gold in November, and clear post-typhoon days deliver the year's first snow-capped Fuji photos.

Prime season: the driest air, near-daily visible Fuji, and outdoor onsen (rotenburo) at their steamy best. December–February is when the postcard shots happen.

Don't miss

  • The Hakone loop: railway, ropeway and Lake Ashi cruise
  • Black eggs at steaming Owakudani valley
  • Hakone Open-Air Museum
  • An onsen ryokan night with kaiseki dinner
  • Chureito Pagoda & Lake Kawaguchiko for Fuji photos

Getting there

From Shinjuku: Odakyu Romancecar to Hakone-Yumoto, ~85 min. Hakone Free Pass covers the whole loop.
