鹿Kansai

Nara

Bowing deer, a giant Buddha, and Japan's first capital

Best time to visit: Mar–May, Oct–Nov

Before Kyoto, there was Nara — Japan's capital in the 8th century and home to some of its oldest and largest wooden buildings. Todai-ji's Great Buddha Hall houses a 15-meter bronze Buddha that still stops first-time visitors mid-step. Around it, 1,200 wild sika deer roam Nara Park and famously bow for shika senbei crackers.

Most people do Nara as a half-day trip from Kyoto or Osaka, but staying until evening — when the deer settle and lanterns light Kasuga Taisha's stone paths — is one of Kansai's best-kept secrets.

Through the seasons

Deer graze under blossoms in Nara Park, and Mt. Yoshino — Japan's most famous sakura mountain with 30,000 trees — is a doable side trip.

Early August's Nara Tokae fills the park with 20,000 candle lanterns at dusk — magical, and cooler than daytime sightseeing.

Deer 'calling' ceremonies resume, ginkgo carpets Todai-ji's lawns gold, and late November maples at Tanzan Shrine reward a short excursion.

Yamayaki in late January sets Mt. Wakakusa's dead grass ablaze after fireworks — one of Japan's strangest, most photogenic festivals.

In pictures

Don't miss

  • Todai-ji Temple and the Great Buddha
  • Feeding (and bowing with) deer in Nara Park
  • Kasuga Taisha's 3,000 lanterns
  • Naramachi old merchant quarter
  • Mochi-pounding show at Nakatanidou

Getting there

From Kyoto: Kintetsu express, ~35 min. From Osaka-Namba: Kintetsu, ~40 min.
