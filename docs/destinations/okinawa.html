海Okinawa

Okinawa

Coral seas, island time and a culture all its own

Best time to visit: Apr–Jun, Oct–Nov (avoid Aug–Sep typhoons)

Okinawa was the independent Ryukyu Kingdom for 450 years, and it still feels like a different country: its own cuisine (goya champuru, Okinawa soba, taco rice), its own music, and some of the clearest water in Asia. Naha's Kokusai Street and the restored Shuri Castle anchor the main island, while Churaumi Aquarium's whale sharks are a family favorite.

The real magic lies offshore. The Kerama Islands — 35 minutes by ferry — have beaches that rival the Maldives, and Ishigaki and Miyako, a short flight south, offer world-class snorkeling and diving over living coral.

Through the seasons

Japan's earliest sakura (late January!) and calm seas from April; Golden Week aside, spring beats summer for beach weather without typhoon risk.

Peak beach and diving season with 30°C water; Eisa drum dance festivals roll through towns in August. Book everything early and watch typhoon forecasts.

Sea stays swimmable into November, crowds thin after October, and the great tug-of-war festival in Naha (October) pulls a 40-ton rope through downtown.

20°C 'winter' — whale-watching season (Jan–Mar) off the Kerama Islands, and the illumination-lit Churaumi area stays open late. No beach, but no crowds either.

In pictures

Don't miss

  • Churaumi Aquarium's giant whale shark tank
  • Kerama Islands snorkeling day trip
  • Shuri Castle and Naha's Kokusai Street
  • Okinawa soba and goya champuru at local shokudo
  • Ishigaki's Kabira Bay and star-sand beaches

Getting there

Fly Tokyo→Naha, ~2h45m; direct flights from Seoul, Taipei and Hong Kong. A rental car is near-essential outside Naha.
