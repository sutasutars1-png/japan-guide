The Perfect 7-Day Japan Itinerary for First-Timers

Tokyo, Hakone, Kyoto, Nara and Osaka in one smooth week — the classic Golden Route, with exact train moves and where to sleep each night.

Days 1–3: Tokyo

Land, get an IC card (Suica/Pasmo on your phone works for foreigners) and base yourself near the Yamanote Line — Shinjuku, Shibuya or Ueno all work. Day 1: recover with an easy evening in Shibuya and dinner at a department-store food hall. Day 2: Asakusa's Senso-ji in the morning, Ueno Park, then Akihabara or teamLab (book ahead). Day 3: Meiji Shrine, Harajuku, Shibuya Sky at sunset, and a night out in Shinjuku's Omoide Yokocho alleys.

Day 4: Hakone onsen night

Send your big luggage ahead to Kyoto with takkyubin (ask your hotel; ~¥2,500 per bag) and travel light. Take the Odakyu Romancecar from Shinjuku, do the Hakone loop — railway, ropeway over Owakudani, pirate ship across Lake Ashi — and check into an onsen ryokan for a kaiseki dinner. This is the night most travelers remember longest.

Days 5–6: Kyoto (+ half-day Nara)

Shinkansen from Odawara to Kyoto (~2h). Day 5: Fushimi Inari as early as you can wake, Kiyomizu-dera, then Gion at dusk. Day 6 morning: Kintetsu train to Nara (35 min) for the Great Buddha and bowing deer; return for Arashiyama's bamboo grove in the late afternoon, when tour groups thin out.

Day 7: Osaka

Thirty minutes by train from Kyoto. Spend your last day eating: Kuromon Market for lunch, Osaka Castle in the afternoon, Dotonbori's neon for a takoyaki-and-kushikatsu dinner. Fly out of Kansai Airport (KIX), 40 minutes from Namba — flying into Tokyo and out of Osaka saves a half day of backtracking.

Questions travelers ask

Do I need a JR Pass for this itinerary?

Usually no. After the 2023 price increase, individual tickets (Tokyo→Kyoto→Osaka one-way) are cheaper than a 7-day pass for this route. Buy Shinkansen tickets individually or via SmartEX.

How much cash should I carry?

Cards and IC payments now work almost everywhere in cities, but keep ¥10,000–20,000 for shrines, small ramen shops and rural buses. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards.
